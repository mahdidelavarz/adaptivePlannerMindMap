## Current Evidence Buckets

### 1. Overdue-list shame

Users describe overdue task lists as emotionally heavy, cluttered, or discouraging.

Related hypothesis:

Unresolved tasks can create avoidance and planner abandonment.

### 2. Restart friction

Users may abandon the whole planning system after falling behind instead of calmly updating it.

Related hypothesis:

A non-judgmental reconcile flow may help users restart.

### 3. Tool switching

Users often move between Notion, Todoist, TickTick, paper, and mental planning after the previous system becomes too heavy.

Related hypothesis:

The problem is not only missing features; it may be the emotional cost of recovery after failure.
